📍 Places & Eats

San Jose · San Francisco · Curated picks

Sightglass Coffeemust
SF's best cafe · SoMa · Stunning industrial warehouse space
▾
📍 7th St & Folsom, SoMa, San Francisco
🕐 Mon–Fri 7am–5pm · Sat–Sun 8am–5pm

A converted warehouse turned cathedral of coffee — two stories, skylights, and some of the most thoughtful espresso in SF. Already on the Day 4 itinerary, but the space itself is worth lingering in. Excellent single-origins and seasonal filter options.

📍 Maps 📸 Instagram
Blue Bottle Coffee
Ferry Building · The SF original · Minimalist & excellent
▾
📍 1 Ferry Building Marketplace, San Francisco
🕐 Daily 7am–6pm

Blue Bottle started in Oakland and became the face of the third-wave coffee movement worldwide. The Ferry Building spot is the most atmospheric — order at the counter and take it outside by the bay. Pair with Acme Bread or Cowgirl Creamery from the adjacent stalls.

📍 Maps 📸 Instagram
Chromatic Coffee
San Jose's best roaster · Race St · Specialty single-origins
▾
📍 42 Race St, San Jose, CA 95126
🕐 Mon–Fri 7am–6pm · Sat–Sun 8am–5pm

San Jose's finest specialty coffee roaster. Light, clean brewing with excellent single-origin pour-overs and an airy, well-designed space. The ideal slow-morning spot before heading out — and completely walkable from most downtown hotels.

📍 Maps 📸 Instagram
Original Joe'smust
San Jose institution since 1956 · Italian-American classics · Open kitchen
▾
📍 301 S 1st St, San Jose, CA 95113
🕐 Mon–Thu 11am–10pm · Fri–Sat 11am–11pm · Sun 11am–9pm

A San Jose landmark since 1956. Art Deco interior with an open kitchen, white tablecloths, and classic Italian-American cooking done right. Order Joe's Special (ground beef, spinach & eggs) or the veal piccata. The bar up front is great for a pre-dinner drink.

📍 Maps
San Pedro Square Market
San Jose food hall · Casual · Multiple vendors, craft beer
▾
📍 87 N San Pedro St, San Jose, CA 95110
🕐 Daily 11am–9pm (varies by vendor)

San Jose's best food hall — indoor/outdoor market with vendors covering pizza, Korean BBQ, Thai, burgers, and craft beer. Great for a casual lunch or early dinner. Lively atmosphere especially on weekend evenings. Totally low-key and delicious.

📍 Maps
Flour + Watermust
SF · Mission · Best pasta in the city · Book ahead on Resy
▾
📍 2401 Harrison St, Mission District, San Francisco
🕐 Dinner only · Tue–Sun from 5:30pm · Book on Resy

The best pasta restaurant in San Francisco — possibly the best on the west coast. House-made noodles in a warm, relaxed Mission space. Book at least a week in advance. Must order: tagliatelle al ragù, and whatever the seasonal pasta is on that night.

📍 Maps 📸 Instagram
Santana Row
San Jose · Upscale outdoor dining & shopping · Evening stroll
▾
📍 377 Santana Row, San Jose, CA 95128
🕐 Shops ~10am–9pm · Restaurants open late

San Jose's most upscale outdoor district — wide tree-lined avenues with boutique shops, rooftop bars, and excellent restaurants (Sino, The Table, Straits). Great for a relaxed evening walk. About 10 minutes from downtown by Uber.

📍 Maps
Westfield Valley Fair
SJ · Massive indoor mall · Apple Store, Nordstrom, 200+ stores
▾
📍 2855 Stevens Creek Blvd, Santa Clara, CA 95050
🕐 Mon–Sat 10am–9pm · Sun 11am–7pm

One of the largest malls in the Bay Area, right next to Santana Row. Apple Store, Nordstrom, Zara, H&M, Uniqlo, and 200+ more. The food court is surprisingly solid. Worth combining with a Santana Row visit — both are in the same complex.

📍 Maps
Willow Glen
SJ neighbourhood · Lincoln Ave · Boutiques, bakeries & patios
▾
📍 Lincoln Ave, Willow Glen, San Jose, CA
🕐 Stroll any time · Cafes open from 7am

San Jose's most charming neighbourhood. Lincoln Ave is lined with independent boutiques, bakeries, wine bars, and locals sipping coffee on patios. Completely unpretentious and walkable. Great for a slow Sunday morning or a recovery stroll after the wedding.

📍 Maps
Winchester Mystery House
Victorian mansion · 160 rooms · Staircases to nowhere
▾
📍 525 S Winchester Blvd, San Jose, CA 95128
🕐 Daily 9am–5pm · Tours from $45

Sarah Winchester (of the rifle family) built this mansion obsessively for 38 years — doors opening to walls, staircases going into ceilings, windows on the floor. 160 rooms and 10,000 windows. Bizarre, fascinating, and completely one-of-a-kind. Allow 2 hours.

💡 First-Timer USA Tips

Everything you need to know before you go

You're going to have an incredible trip. No car needed at all — both San Jose (Uber/Lyft) and NYC (subway) are fully manageable. These tips will make everything smoother and safer.

✈️ Before You Leave Singapore ▼
  • Get an eSIM before you fly — Airalo app, buy a US data plan (10–15GB for ~SGD 15–25). Works the moment you land at SFO.
  • Apply for ESTA now — esta.cbp.dhs.gov. ~USD 21, approved in minutes, valid 2 years. Required for all SG passport holders.
  • Notify your bank and credit cards you're travelling to USA — prevents cards being blocked.
  • Download before flying: Google Maps (save offline maps for SF and NYC!), Uber, Citymapper (NYC), OpenTable / Resy (restaurant bookings), TodayTix (Broadway).
  • US plug: Type A (two flat pins), 110V. Singapore plugs don't fit. Bring a universal adapter.
  • Book popular NYC restaurants now — Lilia, Don Angie, Via Carota fill up weeks in advance. Use Resy or OpenTable.
  • Bring some USD cash for: tips, cash-only restaurants (Peter Luger, La Taqueria), street food, markets.
🚆 Getting Around — Zero Driving ▼
  • San Jose: Uber and Lyft everywhere. San Jose is not walkable — don't try to walk between places. Always hail from the app.
  • SF Day Trip: Caltrain from San Jose Diridon → SF 4th & King (~55 min, ~$10 via app or Clipper card)
  • SF City: Muni buses and trams. Clipper card = Singapore EZ-Link equivalent. Uber fills any gaps.
  • NYC Subway: Just tap your contactless credit/debit card or phone at the turnstile — OMNY system. $2.90 per ride, 24/7. No MetroCard needed.
  • From JFK: AirTrain to Jamaica → E or A train into Manhattan. ~45 min, ~$10. Straightforward. Google Maps has exact directions.
  • Citymapper is the best app for NYC subway navigation — real-time, tells you exactly which door to stand near.
💵 Tipping — Non-Optional in the USA ▼
  • Restaurants: 18–20% minimum. Staff wages are legally lower in the US because tips make up the difference. This is not optional.
  • Many restaurants now show "suggested" tip amounts on the bill — check if service is already added before tipping again.
  • Bars: $1–2 per drink when ordering at the bar. Keeps service fast and bartenders happy.
  • Hotel housekeeping: $2–5 per night, left on the pillow or in a tip envelope. Optional but appreciated.
  • Uber/Lyft: 10–15% tip in the app after the ride. Optional but common.
🛍️ Money & Shopping ▼
  • Sales tax is NOT on price tags — added at checkout. NYC rate is ~8.875%. What you see on the shelf is not what you pay.
  • NYC clothing & footwear under $110 per item is tax-free — great for shopping. No such exemption in SF.
  • Use a card with no foreign transaction fees — DBS Visa Debit, YouTrip, or Wise card save you money on every purchase.
  • Most places are card/contactless. Some legendary spots are cash only: Peter Luger Steak House, La Taqueria (SF), some street vendors.
🛡️ Safety — Both Cities ▼
  • Both cities are completely safe in all the areas in this itinerary. Millions of tourists every year.
  • SF: Avoid the Tenderloin neighbourhood (between Union Square and Civic Center). It's small and easy to route around.
  • SF: Some blocks around Civic Center / parts of Market Street can feel uncomfortable — just keep walking.
  • NYC: Subway is generally safe. Late at night, stand in the well-lit centre of the platform near the conductor's position.
  • Keep bags zipped and in front of you in crowded subway stations and on busy streets.
  • If approached aggressively, it's completely fine to walk away firmly without engaging.
  • Emergency: 911 — police, ambulance, fire. Same as calling 999/995 in Singapore.
  • Share live location with family back home via Google Maps — peace of mind for everyone.
🗣️ Culture & Language ▼
  • Say "restroom" or "bathroom" — not "toilet".
  • Portions are enormous — split mains at most restaurants, or ask for a "to-go box" for leftovers. No one will judge you.
  • Public restrooms are rare — use Starbucks (no purchase needed), hotel lobbies, museum bathrooms.
  • Indoors are heavily air-conditioned — restaurants, shops, subway. Bring a light layer even in mild March weather.
  • In NYC: walk on the right side of the pavement and don't stop suddenly in the middle of the sidewalk. New Yorkers move fast.
  • People are genuinely friendly if you ask for directions — don't be shy.
🌤️ Weather — March ▼
  • San Jose: Mild, 14–20°C. Can be sunny. Light jacket is enough.
  • SF: Cooler, 12–17°C, possibly rainy and windy. SF fog is real. Bring a proper jacket and a small umbrella.
  • NYC: Early spring, 5–14°C. Can be cold or surprisingly mild. Bring a warm jacket with layers underneath.
  • Most important: Comfortable broken-in walking shoes. You will walk 10–15km per day. Fashionable but uncomfortable shoes will ruin you by day 3.
🎁 Best Free Things on This Trip ▼
  • SF: Clarion Alley murals · Ferry Building waterfront · Dolores Park · Alamo Square (Painted Ladies) · Apple Park Visitor Center
  • NYC: High Line · Brooklyn Bridge walk · Central Park · Staten Island Ferry (Statue of Liberty views) · Chelsea Galleries · The Oculus · DUMBO waterfront · Brooklyn Bridge lit at night
  • Some of the very best moments on this trip cost absolutely nothing.
